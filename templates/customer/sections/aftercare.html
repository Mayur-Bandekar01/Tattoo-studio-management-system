Important — How to Heal Your Tattoo

Taking care of your tattoo is very important to keep it looking its best. If you don't follow these steps, your tattoo might fade or you could have skin problems. Make sure to follow your artist's advice exactly.

The First 24 Hours

1

Protect Your New Tattoo

Your artist will wrap your tattoo. Keep it on for 2–4 hours. Don't take it off early.

2

Cleaning Your Tattoo

Use warm water and mild soap. Pat it dry with a clean paper towel — keeping it clean is very important.

3

Moisturizing

Apply a very thin layer of tattoo lotion. Your skin needs to breathe, so don't use too much lotion.

First 2 Weeks — Healing Time

What to Do

  • Wash 2–3 times every day
  • Keep the skin moisturized
  • Wear loose, clean clothes
  • Keep your surroundings clean

What NOT to Do

  • DON'T scratch or pick at it
  • NO swimming or soaking in water
  • NO direct sunlight
  • NO heavy exercise or gym sessions

Keeping it Great Forever

Protect from Sun

Always use SPF 50+ sunscreen. Sunlight causes ink to fade over time.

Skin Health

Use lotion every day. Dry skin makes tattoos look dull and can cause fading.

Artist Check-up

Come back after 4 months. We can touch up any areas that need it.

Your Healing Journey

01–03

Normal

Early Healing

Some redness and swelling is normal. It might ooze a little at first.

04–07

Peeling

Peeling Phase

Your skin will start to peel. Don't pick at it; let it fall off naturally.

04+

Healed

Fully Healed

Colors are set. The skin keeps healing for 3 months. Your tattoo is safe!

When to See a Doctor

Swelling that gets worse after 3 days
Pus or a bad smell from the tattoo
Fever or chills
Pain that keeps getting worse
Red lines spreading from the tattoo
A rash that won't go away
