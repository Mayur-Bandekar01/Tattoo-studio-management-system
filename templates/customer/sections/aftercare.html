Important — Read Before Your Session!

Proper aftercare is essential for tattoo healing and color retention. Ignoring these instructions can lead to infection or fading. Always follow your artist's specific advice too!

Immediately After (0 — 24 Hours)

1

Keep the wrap on

Your artist will wrap your tattoo. Keep it on for 2–4 hours minimum. Do not remove it early!

2

Gently wash with lukewarm water

After removing wrap, wash gently with mild antibacterial soap. Pat dry with a clean paper towel — never rub!

3

Apply thin layer of moisturizer

Use unscented lotion or tattoo aftercare cream. Apply a very thin layer — do not over-moisturize!

First 2 Weeks — Healing Phase

✅ DO These

  • • Wash 2–3 times daily
  • • Moisturize regularly
  • • Wear loose clothing over tattoo
  • • Keep it clean and dry
  • • Stay hydrated — drink water!

❌ AVOID These

  • • Do NOT scratch or pick scabs
  • • No swimming or soaking
  • • No direct sunlight on tattoo
  • • No tight clothing over area
  • • No gym / heavy sweating

Long Term Care

Sun Protection

Always apply SPF 50+ sunscreen on your tattoo before sun exposure. UV rays fade tattoo ink over time.

Moisturize Daily

Keep your tattoo moisturized daily with unscented lotion. Dry skin makes tattoos look dull and faded.

Touch-up Sessions

After 3–6 months, consider a touch-up session to refresh any areas that may have faded during healing.

Healing Timeline

Day 1–3

Fresh

Redness and swelling — completely normal!

Some oozing of ink and plasma is expected. Keep clean!

Day 4–7

Peeling

Skin starts to peel and flake

DO NOT pick or scratch! Let it peel naturally.

Week 2–3

Itchy

Itching and cloudy appearance

Gently tap — never scratch! Looks milky but will clear up.

Week 4+

Healed!

✅ Outer skin healed!

Colors look vivid and sharp. Full deep healing takes 3–4 months.

⚠️ See a Doctor If You Notice:

• Excessive swelling after day 3

• Pus or yellow discharge

• Fever or chills

• Extreme pain that worsens

• Red streaks spreading outward

• Rash or allergic reaction
