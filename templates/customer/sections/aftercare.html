Critical — Healing Protocol

Proper aftercare is essential for masterpiece preservation. Neglecting these protocols can compromise color retention and skin health. Follow your artist's specific guidance religiously.

Immediate After (0 — 24 Hours)

1

Protect the Canvas

Your artist will wrap your tattoo. Keep it on for 2–4 hours. Do not expose the fresh wound prematurely.

2

Purification

Use lukewarm water and mild antibacterial soap. Pat dry with a fresh paper towel — absolute sterility is key.

3

Hydration

Apply a micro-layer of tattoo-specific moisturizer. The skin must breathe; do not suffocate it in ointment.

First 2 Weeks — Regeneration

Standard Protocol

  • Wash 2–3 times daily
  • Maintain hydration levels
  • Wear loose, breathable fabrics
  • Environmental sterility

Strict Prohibitions

  • NO scratching or picking
  • NO aquatic immersion (pools)
  • NO direct photons (sunlight)
  • NO high-impact sweating

Long Term Preservation

UV Resistance

Always deploy SPF 50+ blockers. Photons destroy ink integrity over cycles.

Dermal Matrix

Moisturize daily. Desiccated skin leads to visual degradation and fading.

Calibration

Post 4-month checkup. We refresh any areas that settled unevenly.

Regenerative Timeline

Emergency Alert Protocols:

• Escalating edema after 72 hours

• Purulent discharge or odor

• Systemic pyrexia (fever)

• Intensifying pain cycles

• Erythematous streaks (red lines)

• Persistent allergic reaction
