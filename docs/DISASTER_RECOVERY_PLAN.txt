# Disaster Recovery & Business Continuity Plan: Dragon Tattoos Studio Management System (DTSMS)

## 1. Executive Summary
This document establishes the protocol for recovering the DTSMS operations following a critical failure, such as a localized AWS region outage, catastrophic database corruption, or a ransomware attack. The primary objective is to maintain studio operations with minimal financial loss.

## 2. Recovery Objectives
- **Recovery Time Objective (RTO):** 2 Hours. The system must be fully restored and capable of processing new bookings within two hours of a declared disaster.
- **Recovery Point Objective (RPO):** 1 Hour. In the worst-case scenario, the studio will lose no more than 60 minutes of operational data (e.g., appointments, invoices).

## 3. Backup Strategies
- **Database (MySQL):** Amazon RDS automated snapshots occur daily. Additionally, transaction logs are archived every 15 minutes to an isolated S3 bucket, allowing for Point-in-Time Recovery (PITR).
- **Static Assets:** Reference images and UI assets are hosted in AWS S3 with Cross-Region Replication (CRR) enabled, ensuring that assets remain available even if the primary data center fails.

## 4. Disaster Scenarios and Responses

### 4.1 Database Corruption
- **Trigger:** Invalid data ingestion or accidental mass-deletion via Owner panel.
- **Response:** The engineering team will immediately trigger the RDS Point-in-Time Recovery script, restoring the database state to the minute preceding the corruption event. The frontend will display a "Maintenance Mode" screen during the 15-minute restoration window.

### 4.2 Complete Regional Outage (AWS US-East-1 goes offline)
- **Trigger:** AWS Status dashboard confirms regional failure.
- **Response:** The standby architecture in a secondary region (e.g., US-West-2) is activated via pre-configured Terraform scripts. Route53 DNS traffic is automatically rerouted to the secondary load balancer.

## 5. Manual Fallback Operations
Should the digital infrastructure fail entirely for an extended period, the studio relies on a "Paper-First" fallback protocol. The system automatically emails the Owner a PDF summary of the day's schedule at 7:00 AM every morning. Artists will use this manifest to complete appointments and manually log inventory, which will be retroactively ingested into the DTSMS once service is restored.
